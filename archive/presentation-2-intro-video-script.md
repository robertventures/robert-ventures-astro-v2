## Script

### 1. Hook + intro + bridge (~22s)

> A fixed eight to ten percent a year, for up to ten years. Let me show you how.
>
> I'm Joe Robert, founder and manager of Robert Ventures, and I have been investing for twenty-five years. When you invest in Robert Ventures, you're investing alongside me, on coastal real estate we already own and a Bitcoin reserve held at a federally chartered bank. There is no fund layer in between, and no middlemen taking a cut.

### 2. Outer Banks land (~25s)

> The first asset behind our strategy is land, and specifically, the Outer Banks of North Carolina. It is a two-hundred-mile chain of barrier islands where federal parks and coastal rules hard-cap how much can ever be built. Inventory is finite, and demand keeps coming.
>
> Five million visitors a year. The median property in Dare County now sits at six hundred and twenty thousand, up sixteen percent year over year, and prices have roughly doubled over the last ten years. The model is straightforward: we buy lots, build on them, and sell into the comp, in about a ten-month cycle.

### 3. Digital reserve (~22s)

> The second asset is our Bitcoin reserve. Bitcoin is the only digital asset with a fixed twenty-one million unit cap, which makes it the scarcest monetary asset humans have ever built.
>
> We hold it, we do not trade it. The reserve is custodied at BitGo, which is a federally chartered bank. And the eight to ten percent we pay you is not indexed to the Bitcoin price. It is paid from company cash flows. The reserve is a hedge, not the engine.

### 4. Why the rate works (~18s)

> So how do we actually pay this. It comes down to four pillars: scarce coastal land where prices rise faster than supply, no fund layer between us and you, no leverage on the digital side, and a company that is founder-run instead of committee-run.
>
> Take any one of those away and the math stops working. Stack them together, and eight to ten percent becomes the floor, not the ceiling.

### 5. Track record (~18s)

> This is not a new firm writing its first checks. It is the current vehicle of a team that has been operating for twenty-five years, with one hundred and fifty million in cumulative assets and over two hundred properties behind it.
>
> In this offering specifically, we have raised one point two three million from seventy-six investors, and every one of them was onboarded by hand. One hundred percent on-time distributions to date.

### 6. Cost of waiting (~18s)

> One last frame for you. Picture the same ten thousand dollars, sitting for the same ten years, in four different places. In a savings account at four percent, you would end up with about fifteen thousand. In a five-year CD, around fifteen and a half. In private credit at eight, about twenty-two. With us at ten, just over twenty-seven.
>
> Compounding starts the day you invest, not the day you decide.

### 7. Close (~25s)

> From here, take a look at the questions just below this video. They are the ones investors actually ask us, and you can explore them in whatever order you want. When you are ready, book a ten-minute call with my team, or create your account and come aboard. Either way, I am looking forward to having you with us.

## House style guardrails

- No em-dashes (use periods, commas, colons, parentheses, or split into two sentences).
- Never use "guarantee" or "guaranteed."
- Use "built on," not "backed by."
- Say "property" or "properties," not "home" or "homes," to match the introduction page's vocabulary.
- Joe's title is "founder and manager of Robert Ventures." Do not shorten to "founder."
- Joe's 25-year track record is as an investor. Do not write "started building," "started lending," or other operator framings of his personal history.
- Robert Ventures does not lend money. Do not imply lending in any line.
- Joe is the only speaker on camera. Every line is delivered by him in first person. Do not write narrator-style framing.
- Numbers carry credibility. Avoid framing chips like "why smart investors choose us."
