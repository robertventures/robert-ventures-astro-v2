## Script

### 1. Hook, intro, and bridge (~45s)

> A fixed eight to ten percent a year. For up to ten years. Let me show you how.
>
> I'm Joe Robert, founder and manager of Robert Ventures. I have been an investor for twenty-five years, and I founded Robert Ventures for one reason. Most people who want a fixed rate that actually beats inflation end up either taking too much risk, or paying too many middlemen to get it.
>
> Our answer is one contract. Built on coastal real estate we already own, and a Bitcoin reserve held at a federally chartered bank. No fund layer. No middlemen. The next four minutes are everything underneath it.

### 2. Outer Banks land (~50s)

> The first asset behind your contract is land. The Outer Banks of North Carolina. Two hundred miles of barrier islands where federal parks, coastal setbacks, and septic rules hard-cap how much can ever be built. Inventory is finite. Demand is not.
>
> Five million visitors a year. Two point one billion in tourism spending in 2024. The median Dare County property is six hundred twenty thousand, up sixteen percent year over year. Prices have roughly doubled in the last ten years.
>
> Our build economics are plain. Buy lots for under a hundred thousand. Build for around three fifty. Sell into the comp around five seventy-five. About ten months a cycle. We build a couple at a time, sell, then start the next.

### 3. Digital reserve (~45s)

> The second asset is our digital reserve. Bitcoin, with a small Ethereum allocation. Bitcoin is the only digital asset with a fixed twenty-one million unit cap written into the protocol. The scarcest monetary asset humans have ever built.
>
> Two things matter here. We hold it, we do not trade it. The reserve is custodied at BitGo, a federally chartered qualified custodian, and audited and disclosed in our semi-annual SEC reporting.
>
> And your contract is not indexed to Bitcoin price. Your eight to ten percent is paid from company cash flows. The reserve is a hedge against fiat debasement. It is not the engine.

### 4. Why the rate works (~35s)

> So how do we actually pay this. Four pillars. Scarce coastal land, where prices rise faster than supply. No fund layer between us and you, so no manager fee siphons your yield. No leverage on the digital side, no margin call risk. And we are founder-run, not committee-run.

### 5. Track record (~35s)

> This is not a new firm writing its first checks. This is the current vehicle of a team that has been operating for twenty-five years. One hundred fifty million in cumulative assets. Two hundred plus properties. Fifteen hundred mortgages managed. Forty plus technology investments.
>
> In this specific offering we have raised one point two three million from seventy-six investors, every one of them onboarded by hand. One hundred percent on-time distributions to date. Twenty-seven percent of our investors choose to reinvest when their term ends.

### 6. Cost of waiting (~35s)

> One last frame. Same ten thousand dollars, same ten years, four endings. In a four percent savings account, about fifteen thousand. In a five-year CD, around fifteen and a half. In private credit at eight percent, around twenty-two. With us at ten, just over twenty-seven thousand.
>
> In any single year the gap looks small. Over ten years, it is the difference between treading water and nearly tripling your money. Compounding starts the day you invest, not the day you decide.

### 7. Close (~25s)

> Two ways to move from here. If you're ready, create your account. You'll be hand-walked through onboarding the same way our last seventy-six investors were. If you still have questions, book a ten-minute call. We will answer them directly.
>
> That's the page. I'll see you on the inside.

## House style guardrails

- No em-dashes (use periods, commas, colons, parentheses, or split into two sentences).
- Never use "guarantee" or "guaranteed."
- Use "built on," not "backed by."
- Say "property" or "properties," not "home" or "homes," to match the introduction page's vocabulary.
- Joe's title is "founder and manager of Robert Ventures." Do not shorten to "founder."
- Joe's 25-year track record is as an investor. Do not write "started building," "started lending," or other operator framings of his personal history.
- Joe founded Robert Ventures. The firm makes one offering and the offering is structured as one contract. Do not write "we built one contract" or "we built this offering."
- Joe is the only speaker on camera. Every line in the script is delivered by him in first person. Do not write narrator-style framing like "here is who is behind it" or "meet Joe Robert," because there is no third party introducing him.
- Numbers carry credibility. Avoid framing chips like "why smart investors choose us."
